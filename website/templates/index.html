What is Hobson's Conduit Trust?

Hobson’s Conduit Trust was established by Thomas Hobson to maintain good quality water supply from Nine Wells into Cambridge.

In the early 1600’s, Cambridge was regularly affected by plague and other fatal infections thought to be caused by contaminated water. A constant supply of fresh drinking water was considered necessary to protect the population.

Four miles away, the Nine Wells springs in Great Shelford was a source of clean water. The authorities from Cambridge Town and University collaborated with the Lord of the Manor in Trumpington to create a ‘new river’ to divert some of this fresh water into Cambridge.

In 1631, Thomas Hobson, whose income came from transporting goods to and from London, bequeathed land to fund and maintain this public water supply - hence the association with his name. Hobson’s Conduit Trust is now a registered charity, that was established to have rights over the stream and to maintain it in good order for the town and the University.

Where is Hobson's Conduit?

The water in Hobson’s Conduit runs above ground from the Nine Wells Nature Reserve, south of Cambridge (CB22 5JY). The stream passes west of Addenbrooke’s Hospital (CB2 0QQ), and Trumpington Park, under Long Road and Brookland’s Avenue, beside the western end of the Botanic Garden (CB2 1JE). Finally the water flows by Brookside, terminating at the Conduit Head (CB2 1PY). From here the conduit runs in underground channels but the water is still visible along the runnels in Trumpington Street and in some college ponds.
